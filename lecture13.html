Week 13 — Professional, Telephone and Dining Etiquette

This lecture covered why professional behavior and etiquette are necessary in working and social settings. We learned that socialization instructs us in how to learn new roles, and that etiquette is a "script" for showing others that we are respectful and can be trusted. The lecturer put particular emphasis on first impressions, personal grooming and appearance, and the effect that minor behaviors (greeting, handshake, eye contact) have on others' judgment of our competence and trustworthiness. The session also covered telephone etiquette practice and definitive rules for polite eating, showing how good manners help in professional relations and confidence.

Key Learnings

  • I learned that etiquette is practical and can be learned:
    • polish your personal image
    • dress appropriately.
    • smile and engage in eye contact and assertive body language because people form impressions in an instant.
    • Learn to recall names, accept compliments with appreciation, and handle business cards with respect.

  • Phone etiquette matters:
    • answer within a few rings.
    • ask before placing one on hold.
    • ake effective messages (name, number, time, action), and be courteous in call transfers.

  • At meals :

    • follow simple rules napkin on lap, pass dishes to the left, keep elbows off the table, don't talk with your mouth full, and silence your phone because table manners reflect your professionalism.
