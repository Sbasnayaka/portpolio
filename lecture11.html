Week 11 — Email Writing


We were taught in this lecture how to write clear, professional emails for work and academic use. Madam covered the general structure of an email (subject line, salutation, opening line, body, closure, and sign-off) and touched upon when to use formal or informal tone. We practiced writing short emails in common scenarios like requesting feedback, sending out assignments, arranging meetings, and were taught basic rules like keeping them concise, having a useful subject heading, sending documents in proper format, and previewing before sending.


Key Learnings

I learned that a good email starts with a clear subject and a polite greeting, states the purpose in the first sentence, and uses short paragraphs with a clear call to action.

  • choose formal language for teachers/employers
  • Keep emails concise, have a useful subject heading, send documents in proper format, and preview before sending.
  • attach files and mention them in the body
  • include contact details in the signature
  • always proofread for tone, grammar, and spelling
